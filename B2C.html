Problems

Medly is a full-service pharmacy delivering prescriptions, advice, savings, and customer support to help patients stay on track.


The Medly IOS Appâ€Šâ€”â€Šfirst designed in 2018, struggled to scale alongside the hyper-growth of the company. Fundamental usability was challenged. Disparate features and experiments competed for focus. App reliability and performance issues increased tremendously.


The Medly iOS app does not represent the Medly brand as a digital-first technology solution to prescription delivery. This creates frustrating experiences for our patients, heavy operational overhead for our internal teams, and a diluted value proposition for our partners. Because many of our internal services do not communicate with one another, patients are unable to perform basic and critical tasks, creating the need for manual solutions. Simultaneously, key competitors are strengthening their offerings in the marketplace, led by Alto and Capsule. This amplifies the need to bring a robust, patient-centric digital solution to market swiftly.

I also QA the development outcome before every release.

Competitive Analysis

I tested the app features and service from the competitors, compared it with Medly app to define what features need to be improved and prioritized.


Workshop

I faciliated a couple of workshops with cross-funtional team leaders to gather ideas on how to let patients manage their meds, and how to improve the checkout process. PM and I balanced between the user goals and business goals, prioritised and negotiated features for launch and beyond.




Design Process

User Flow Mapping

To begin the design process, we first categorized the ideas from the workshop, laid out of the major features we want to include in the next version. The flow is designed to bring a more streamlined process with less steps and clear CTAs to the patients, allow them manage their medications easily and checkout without any confusion.

Wireframing

Following the user flow mapping, I used the wireframes in Miro to test multiple ideas of layouts, considered about where is a best fit location for each feature and CTA, and discussed with PM and design director to gather more feedbacks.
